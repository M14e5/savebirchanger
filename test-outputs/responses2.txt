The proposal to build 480 homes across Birchanger and Stansted does not meet the criteria set out in the National Planning Policy Framework (NPPF) Paragraph 155. This development fails sustainability tests and undermines the essential purposes of the Green Belt, which deserves protection.

1. **Green Belt Compliance**: Contrary to any suggestion that this land represents a "grey belt," it clearly fulfills important Green Belt functions. The land prevents the merging of Birchanger with Stansted and preserves the historical setting of St Mary's Church, a 12th-century landmark. 3. **Undeliverable "Golden Rules"**: The developer’s commitments lack clarity and binding assurance. While land for a new school may be promised, there is no mention of who will finance construction or staffing needs. 4. **Infrastructure Overload**: The area's existing infrastructure is patently insufficient to accommodate additional pressure from new homes. Forest Hall School is already oversubscribed and lacks facilities such as a sixth form to support educational needs of future residents' children. Local healthcare services face similar strain; GP surgeries are overwhelmed and unable to absorb more patients effectively. B1383/Cambridge Road frequently suffers gridlock without funded plans for significant upgrades.

Based on these critical assessments, this development should be refused approval as it does not align with NPPF mandates nor provide enforceable guarantees through Section 106 agreements prior to occupation. These factors collectively confirm that the proposal is unsuitable for this location which currently serves vital community roles as part of the Green Belt landscape.

The current traffic situation is equally untenable. Each morning, the B1383/Cambridge Road becomes a gridlock nightmare with no funded upgrades in sight. The thought of adding approximately 1,180 daily car trips from this new development raises significant concerns about how our roadways will cope. With transportation options lacking—no Sunday bus service and the train station over two kilometers away without proper pedestrian access—residents will be overwhelmingly car-dependent.

Such car dependency directly contradicts the sustainability principles outlined in NPPF Paragraph 155, which this proposal fails decisively. The absence of reliable and comprehensive public transport further underscores that this location is unsuitable for the dense development being proposed.

On matters of Green Belt objectives, the land in question serves vital purposes: it effectively prevents the merging of Birchanger and Stansted while safeguarding ancient woodland areas. This crucial role reinforces its unsuitability for development under NPPF guidelines, as it undeniably fulfills important Green Belt functions by maintaining village identities and protecting natural heritage.

Furthermore, assurances from developers regarding education infrastructure are tenuous at best. While land may be offered for new school facilities, there are no clear commitments on who will finance or staff them. Without legally binding Section 106 agreements ensuring these provisions are met before any occupation begins, promises remain speculative and undeliverable.

Given these pressing concerns—overstretched schools, insufficient transportation options, and essential Green Belt protections—I urge you to refuse this application outright. It fails both the sustainability test and any practical measures to address existing infrastructural deficits adequately.
