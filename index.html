Why We Must Save Birchanger

City & Country proposes a massive development on the land between Stansted Mountfitchet and Birchanger. This development, marketed under the guise of providing "much-needed homes," threatens to destroy our precious Green Belt land and merge Birchanger, Stansted, and Bishop's Stortford into one continuous urban sprawl.

The Devastating Impact

This isn't just about houses - it's about the fundamental change to our way of life and environment. Here's what we stand to lose:

  • Loss of Agricultural Land: Productive farmland that has sustained our community for generations will be concreted over, affecting local food production and rural economy.
  • Wildlife Habitat Destruction: The development threatens local wildlife corridors and habitats, including those of protected species that rely on these green spaces.
  • Increased Flooding Risk: Converting permeable land to concrete will exacerbate existing drainage problems and increase flood risk for current residents.
  • Traffic Chaos: Already congested roads will become gridlocked, with increased pollution and safety concerns, especially near schools.
  • Loss of Community: The unique character and close-knit nature of Birchanger will be lost as we're absorbed into an urban sprawl.
  • Precedent for Further Development: Once this "grey belt" development is approved, it opens the door for more Green Belt destruction across Essex.

The "Grey Belt" Deception

Don't be fooled by the term "grey belt" - this arable Green Belt land that has protected our countryside and our communities for decades and which is still actively farmed. The government's new policy is being exploited by developers to build on previously protected land in areas where they can see the most profit. They claim to offer 50% affordable housing, but how affordable will that be? And at what cost to our environment and community? If their goal was affordable then why have they not offered to build their development on the many brownfield sites available?

The Truth About "Grey Belt"

The developer claims this land doesn't meet Green Belt purposes, but it clearly:

  • Prevents urban sprawl between Birchanger, Stansted, and Bishop's Stortford
  • Safeguards the countryside from encroachment
  • Preserves the setting and character of historic villages
  • Assists in urban regeneration by encouraging development elsewhere

Birchanger

Birchanger is more than just a place on the map - it's a historic Essex village with a unique character, stunning countryside views, and a strong community spirit that has thrived for centuries. This is what we're fighting to preserve:

Historic St Mary's Church

Our beautiful 12th-century church stands as a testament to Birchanger's long history, with its Anglo-Saxon origins and peaceful churchyard providing a sanctuary for both worship and wildlife.

Birchanger Wood

Our 69-acre ancient woodland is rich in wildlife and birch trees. The name 'Birchanger' itself means 'wooded slope with birch trees' - a heritage spanning over 1000 years.

The Village Green

The heart of our community, where village f√™tes, cricket matches, and gatherings have taken place for generations. This precious open space defines our village character.

Countryside Views

The rolling fields and hedgerows surrounding Birchanger have shaped our landscape for centuries, providing food, employment, and the rural character that makes our village special.

The Three Willows

Our historic village pub serves as a focal point of community life, representing the traditional Essex village character and social heritage we're fighting to preserve.

Historic Footpaths

Our network of ancient footpaths and bridleways connects communities and provides peaceful routes through beautiful countryside - a precious resource for health and wellbeing.
