A) Developer (Pre-application) Consultation Response

For City & Country ‚Äì deadline 2 October 2025

A1. Ultra-quick "survey form" answers

Copy/paste into webform boxes:

Q: What do you think of our proposals?

I oppose building homes on this Green Belt site at [SITE NAME]. The land does strongly serve Green Belt purposes: it checks sprawl, helps keep settlements separate, and contributes to the setting of [HISTORIC AREA/ASSETS]. Labeling it "grey belt" is not evidenced. The scheme harms openness, landscape character, and local ecology, and is not a sustainably located extension.

Q: What are your priorities for the area?

Protect the Green Belt's openness and settlement gaps; focus new homes on brownfield and truly sustainable locations first; secure genuinely additional affordable housing (not just re-badged numbers), safe walking/cycling routes, and infrastructure that actually arrives before occupation.

Q: What type of homes are needed locally?

If more homes are needed, they should be delivered on brownfield or non-Green Belt sites within or immediately next to existing centres, with family-sized rentals, social rent, and key-worker homes‚Äînot by releasing functioning Green Belt.

Q: Any other comments?

The "grey belt" claim is unproven. The site makes a high/clear contribution to Green Belt purposes; no defensible boundary exists; and the proposal relies on car travel. The "Golden Rules" (extra affordable, new green space, infrastructure, biodiversity) are not evidenced to be deliverable or secured. Please withdraw and pursue brownfield alternatives.

Notes & Tips

Keep supporters on solid ground

‚úÖ Stick to material planning issues:

  • Green Belt purposes, openness, landscape/heritage
  • Transport, ecology, flooding
  • Design, infrastructure, policy conflict
  • Alternatives assessment

‚ùå Avoid non-material points:

  • House prices
  • Developer profits
  • Who lives there
  • Loss of a private view

üí° Best Practices:

  • Be polite and factual
  • Add local specifics (photos, measurements, route timings)
  • If you cite stats, reference the source or keep it qualitative
  • Customize templates with your personal observations and evidence
